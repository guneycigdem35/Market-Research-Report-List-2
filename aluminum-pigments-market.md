<p><p>Aluminum pigments are divided into two main types: floating and non-floating. Floating aluminum pigments are lightweight and tend to stay suspended in water-based systems, making them ideal for applications like automotive finishes and printing inks. On the other hand, non-floating aluminum pigments are heavier and settle at the bottom of the coating, providing a different effect and appearance. Non-floating pigments are commonly used in applications like industrial coatings and plastics. Both types offer unique properties and benefits depending on the desired end-use application.</p></p>
