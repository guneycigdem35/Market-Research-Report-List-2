<p><strong>The Automotive Grade POM Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Homopolymer POM</li><li>Copolymer POM</li></ul></p>
<p><p>POM, or polyoxymethylene, is a type of engineering thermoplastic that is commonly used in automotive applications due to its durability and resistance to wear and tear. There are two main types of POM used in the automotive industry: homopolymer POM and copolymer POM. </p><p>Homopolymer POM is a type of POM with a linear structure, which gives it high tensile strength and stiffness. Copolymer POM, on the other hand, is a type of POM with a branched structure, which gives it improved resistance to thermal and chemical degradation. Both types of POM are commonly used in automotive components such as gears, bearings, and fuel system components.</p></p>
