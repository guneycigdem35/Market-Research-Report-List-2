<p><p>Continuous non-metallic fibers, such as basalt fiber, are long fibers that are uniform in composition and structure, providing high strength and flexibility. These fibers are used in applications requiring continuous reinforcement, such as in composite materials. Discrete non-metallic fibers, on the other hand, are shorter fibers that are randomly oriented and provide added strength and toughness to materials. They are commonly utilized in applications where continuous reinforcement is not necessary, such as in construction materials or textiles.</p></p>
