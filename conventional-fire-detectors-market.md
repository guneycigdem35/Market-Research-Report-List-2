<p><strong>The Conventional Fire Detectors Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Heat Detector Type</li><li>Ionization Detector Type</li><li>Photoelectric Detector Type</li><li>Ionization/Photoelectric Detector Type</li><li>Other</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Conventional fire detectors come in various types, each serving a specific purpose in fire detection. Heat detector types are designed to sense changes in temperature, while ionization detector types are sensitive to smoke particles produced by fast-flaming fires. Photoelectric detector types use light sensors to detect smoke particles produced by slow-smoldering fires. Ionization/photoelectric detector types combine the features of both ionization and photoelectric detectors for comprehensive fire detection. Other types of conventional fire detectors may include multi-sensor detectors or gas detectors, offering additional capabilities in fire detection.</p></p>
