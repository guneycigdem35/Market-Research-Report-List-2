<p><p>Diesel Exhaust Fluid (DEF) is a solution made of urea and deionized water that is used in diesel engines equipped with Selective Catalytic Reduction (SCR) systems to reduce harmful nitrogen oxide emissions. DEF is injected into the exhaust stream of diesel vehicles, where it breaks down into ammonia to help convert NOx emissions into nitrogen and water vapor, which are harmless.</p><p>The Diesel Exhaust Fluid Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 10.7% during the forecast period. The growth of the market can be attributed to the increasing stringency of emission regulations across the globe, which has led to the widespread adoption of SCR technology in diesel vehicles.
