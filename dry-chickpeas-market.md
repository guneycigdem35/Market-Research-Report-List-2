<p><p>Dry chickpeas are broadly categorized into two main market types: Kabuli chickpeas and Desi chickpeas. Kabuli chickpeas are larger, creamier in color, and have a smoother coat, making them ideal for dishes like salads and stews. Desi chickpeas are smaller, darker in color, and have a rough coat, often used in Indian and Middle Eastern cuisine for dishes like curries and soups. Both types offer distinct flavors and textures, catering to different culinary preferences and dishes.</p></p>
