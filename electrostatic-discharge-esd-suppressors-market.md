<p><p>Electrostatic Discharge (ESD) suppressors are devices used to protect electronic components from damage caused by electrostatic discharge. There are three main types of ESD suppressors on the market: diode array, polymer, and TVS. Diode array suppressors are composed of multiple diodes connected in series to provide protection against transient voltage spikes. Polymer suppressors use a conductive polymer material to absorb and dissipate energy from transient voltage events. TVS (Transient Voltage Suppression) suppressors are devices designed to limit transient voltage and protect sensitive components from damage.</p></p>
