<p><p>Insoluble dietary fibers are classified into four main types: cellulose, hemicellulose, lignin, and others. Cellulose, found in plant cell walls, provides rigidity and structure. Hemicellulose aids in the bonding of plant cells and is commonly found in cereal grains. Lignin is a complex polymer that provides strength and support to plant cell walls. Other insoluble dietary fibers include resistant starch and chitin. These fibers cannot be digested by the body and help promote digestive health by adding bulk to stools and aiding in regular bowel movements.</p></p>
