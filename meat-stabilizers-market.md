<p><p>Meat stabilizers are additives used in meat products to improve texture, moisture retention, and shelf life. There are various types of stabilizers used in the meat industry including gums, pectin, carrageenan, gelatin, and others. Gums such as xanthan and guar are commonly used for their thickening and stabilizing properties, while pectin is a natural stabilizer derived from fruits. Carrageenan is extracted from seaweed and helps improve moisture retention. Gelatin, derived from animal collagen, is used for gelling and stabilizing meat products. Other stabilizers may include starches and proteins.</p></p>
