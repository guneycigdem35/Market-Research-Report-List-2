<p><ul><li>Ortho Nitrochlorobenzene</li><li>Meta Nitrochlorobenzene</li><li>Para Nitrochlorobenzene</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Nitrochlorobenzene is classified into three market types based on the position of the nitro and chlorine substituents on the benzene ring - Ortho Nitrochlorobenzene, Meta Nitrochlorobenzene, and Para Nitrochlorobenzene. Ortho Nitrochlorobenzene refers to the compound where the nitro and chlorine groups are adjacent to each other on the benzene ring. Meta Nitrochlorobenzene indicates the nitro and chlorine groups are separated by one carbon atom on the benzene ring. Para Nitrochlorobenzene denotes that the nitro and chlorine groups are located opposite each other on the benzene ring.</p></p>
