<p><p>Phosphorus-based flame retardants are commonly categorized into three types based on their mechanism of action: additive flame retardants, reactive flame retardants, and synergist flame retardants. Additive flame retardants are added to materials to delay ignition and reduce flame spread. Reactive flame retardants chemically bond to materials, providing long-lasting protection. Synergist flame retardants work in combination with other chemicals to enhance the overall flame retardant properties of a material. Each type offers unique advantages in improving fire safety in various applications.</p></p>
