<p><p>Soilless culture, also known as hydroponics, is a method of growing plants without soil, using mineral nutrient solutions in a water solvent. This method allows for greater control over the plant's environment and nutrient intake, leading to increased growth rates and yields. Soilless culture is becoming increasingly popular in commercial agriculture, as it can be practiced in areas with poor soil quality or limited space.</p><p>The Soilless Culture Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 10.6% during the forecast period. The increasing demand for fresh fruits and vegetables, rapid urbanization, and rising awareness about sustainable farming practices are driving the growth of the soilless culture market. Additionally, advancements in technology, such as automated nutrient delivery systems and vertical farming techniques, are further fueling the market growth.</p><p>One of the latest trends in the soilless culture market is the integration of aquaponics, a sustainable farming system that combines aquaculture (fish farming) with hydroponics. This innovative method allows for the cultivation of both fish and plants in a symbiotic environment, creating a closed-loop system with minimal waste and maximum efficiency. Overall, the soilless culture market is expected to continue expanding as more growers adopt this efficient and sustainable farming method.</p></p>
